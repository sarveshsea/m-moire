## 2. Triple Bid Process — 2025 Fairness Crisis

### In-House Award Surge

- **2025:** ~50% of photography, integrated, and video production estimates are now awarded internally
- **100% increase** in work awarded to companies within the same agency network/holding company vs prior years
- US DOJ is investigating alleged illegal bid-rigging where agencies pitch in-house units and fix outcomes
- Agencies described as "marking their own homework" — competing in a contest they also judge

### ANA Production Transparency Study Findings

- Non-transparent production practices found at **multiple** ad agencies and holding companies
- **Fewer than half** of respondents require disclosure when agency bids in-house/affiliated production
- **Over 60%** don't require or don't know if production rebates/incentives must be passed back
- Related party transactions were not disclosed, creating competitive bidding issues
- Task force recommendation: all bids should be sent to third-party or advertiser staff FIRST

### Brand-Side Pain Points

- Without solid pre-bid/review processes, purchase orders opened for larger amounts than needed
- Without close PO vs actuals management, significant funds left unrecovered at final billing
- Not enough time to utilize available funds when final billing settles late
- Brands need tools to break free from closed-loop systems that don't align with creative/cost goals

**Source:** [APR - Why Transparency Matters](https://www.aprco.com/resources/why-transparency-matters-in-the-advertising-production-bidding-process), [ANA - Production Transparency Study](https://www.ana.net/content/show/id/pr-2017-production-transparency), [APR 2025 Annual Trends](https://www.aprco.com/resources/2025-annual-trends)

### The AI Token Budget Problem

- **78% of IT leaders** report unexpected charges from AI/consumption-based pricing
- Token costs at production scale: $3,200-$13,000/month in operational spend
- Token costs vary **100x** across models — smart routing cuts costs up to 80%
- Horizon Media's Blu platform: token costs are majority of client onboarding costs
- **No standard AICP line items** for AI compute, token costs, tool subscriptions, or prompt engineering

### AI's Friction Relocation Effect

AI doesn't remove friction — it relocates it from "creation" to "collaboration and review":
- Cheaper per-shot costs => teams explore more creative options
- More iterations => new review bottlenecks
- Edit timeline becomes where VFX-adjacent choices begin (cleanup, reframing, shot replacement)
- New bidding challenge: estimating costs when AI enables 3x more iterations at lower unit cost

### Key Gap Analysis

**ZERO tools offer:**
- AI-powered line-item comparison across competing bids
- Historical rate intelligence / benchmarking
- Bid-to-actual variance prediction from historical data
- Change impact analysis (what happens if I change shoot days?)
- Union rate auto-lookup across SAG-AFTRA/IATSE/DGA with calendar-aware bumps
- AI token cost estimation line items

### ABID Platform Failure

AICP's own digital bid management platform (ABID) went offline December 15, 2024 after 4 years of operation and 2 years of development. Features included:
- All bidders receiving identical information simultaneously
- Trackable DEI spend, overages vs estimates, cost-plus actuals with variances
- Data sortable by region, product, campaign, agency, brand
- $0 ROI on the industry's digital transformation attempt

## 6. Production Company Economics

### Markup vs Actual Profit

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Standard AICP markup | 25% on below-the-line costs |
| Insurance markup | 3% on Sections A-K |
| Travel handling | 15% minimum |
| **Actual net profit per job** | **3-15%** |
| Pass-through items (no markup) | Director fees, talent payments, travel, insurance |

The 25% markup covers: office rent, development costs, business development expenses, insurance overhead, permanent staff salaries. Above-the-line items are typically passed through at cost.

## 7. Bid-to-Actual Variance

### Scope Creep Statistics (Cross-Industry, Applied to Production)

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Projects with scope creep exceeding budget | 85% | PMI |
| Average cost overrun from scope creep | 27% | Industry aggregate |
| Projects with budget overruns from scope creep | 62% | PMI |
| Projects completed on time and budget | Only 29% | Standish Group CHAOS |
| Worst-case scope creep multiplier | 4x initial estimate | IEEE Access |

### Production-Specific Reconciliation

- **EFC methodology:** Compare Estimated Final Cost to actual-to-date per department
- Well-managed productions keep variance close to zero — overages offset by savings
- Industry standard: present final cost report showing estimated vs actual on each AICP line
- $50K variance sounds large until context: it's 2% of a $2.5M budget
- **No production bidding tool currently models or predicts bid-to-actual variance**

### Wrapbook-Saturation Pipeline

First automated bid-to-actual pipeline:
1. Saturation builds AICP bid with templates
2. Wrapbook processes payroll during production
3. Payroll actuals flow back into Saturation automatically
4. Unified cost view without manual reconciliation
5. Department names and GL codes must match for smooth integration

**Source:** [Saturation - Production Cost Report](https://saturation.io/blog/production-cost-report), [Wrapbook - Budget/EFC Dashboard](https://help.wrapbook.com/docs/budget-efc-dashboard-pas)

### Section Breakdown (Current Form)

| Section | Content | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| A-P | Traditional production sections | Pre-production through production |
| Q | Editorial | Former AICE section, reworked |
| R | Social Versions | New — accommodates social deliverables |
| T | Finishing | Former AICE section |
| V | Miscellaneous Editorial | Former AICE section |
| W | Editorial Labor & Creative Fees | Former AICE section |
| X | Reserved | Non-standardized formats for VFX/animation/interactive studios |

### DEI Line Items — Double the Line (DTL)

- Agency/client commits in advance to #doubletheline in bidding specs
- Production company identifies one or more lines to double with BIPOC candidates
- Costs to hire BIPOC candidate covered as agreed budget addition
- AICP also launched standardized demographic reporting methodology (September 2021)
